on the Shortest Day

Because Alaska stretches well beyond (2,500 miles east to west) what one timezone should contain (with the exception of the western Aleutians, which are on Hawaii Time), the Winter Solstice shows the extremes of how Alaskans experience a winter day. In the southeast, the sun is up in time for work, while western communites wait until after noon for the sun's brief time above the horizon. And above the Arctic Circle, the sun won't be up until January or so.
